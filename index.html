FERRARI TEAM DRIVERS

LEWIS HAMILTON

Lewis Hamilton is a British Formula 1 driver widely regarded as one of the greatest in the sport’s history. Born on 7 January 1985, he rose through karting and joined McLaren, winning his first F1 championship in 2008. In 2013, he moved to Mercedes, where he dominated the hybrid era and secured six more world titles, tying the record for most championships. Hamilton also holds records for the most race wins and pole positions. Beyond racing, he is known for activism in diversity, environmental awareness, and animal rights. His influence extends far beyond motorsport, making him a global icon.

CHARLES LECLERC

Charles Leclerc is a Monégasque Formula 1 driver racing for Scuderia Ferrari. Born on 16 October 1997 in Monaco, he rose quickly through junior categories, winning the GP3 Series in 2016 and the Formula 2 Championship in 2017 with dominant performances. Leclerc joined Sauber in 2018 and impressed immediately, earning a promotion to Ferrari in 2019. That year, he won his first two F1 races at Spa and Monza, becoming a fan favourite. Known for his raw speed, strong qualifying pace, and emotional driving style, Leclerc is considered one of the most talented young drivers on the current F1 grid.

Ferrari LaFerrari

The Ferrari LaFerrari is one of the brand’s most advanced hypercars, combining a 6.3-liter V12 engine with an electric motor for a combined output of around 950 horsepower. It uses Ferrari’s HY-KERS hybrid system, inspired by Formula 1 technology, to provide instant torque and improved efficiency without compromising performance. The car can reach speeds over 350 km/h and accelerate from 0 to 100 km/h in under 3 seconds. Built using carbon fiber and advanced aerodynamics, the LaFerrari is extremely rare, with limited production and high collectible value. It represents Ferrari’s peak engineering and innovation philosophy.

Ferrari SF90 Stradale

The Ferrari SF90 Stradale is a plug-in hybrid supercar delivering a combined 1000 horsepower from its twin-turbo V8 engine and three electric motors. It’s the first Ferrari road car with standard all-wheel drive and advanced electric-only driving capability. The SF90 can travel short distances purely on battery power, showcasing Ferrari’s push toward hybrid performance. With a 0–100 km/h time of around 2.5 seconds, it is among the fastest Ferraris ever made. The car features cutting-edge aerodynamics, a futuristic cockpit, and selectable driving modes that blend electric and petrol power seamlessly, making it both extremely fast and technologically advanced.

Ferrari 812 Superfast

The Ferrari 812 Superfast is a front-engine grand tourer powered by a 6.5-liter naturally aspirated V12 producing 789 horsepower. Known for its high-revving engine and brutal acceleration, it reaches 0–100 km/h in about 2.9 seconds, making it one of the quickest V12 GT cars. Ferrari designed the 812 with advanced aerodynamics, rear-wheel steering, and a lightweight body for improved handling and stability at high speeds. Its interior blends luxury with racing inspiration, offering high-quality materials and driver-focused controls. The 812 represents one of the last pure V12 Ferraris without hybrid systems, adding to its emotional appeal.

Ferrari F8 Tributo

The Ferrari F8 Tributo is a mid-engine supercar powered by a 3.9-liter twin-turbo V8 producing 710 horsepower. It pays tribute to Ferrari’s award-winning V8 engines while delivering improved performance and aerodynamics over its predecessor, the 488. The F8 accelerates from 0–100 km/h in about 2.9 seconds and offers responsive handling suitable for both road and track. Ferrari focused on reducing weight and optimizing airflow, making it faster and sharper. Inside, the car features a modern cockpit with updated displays and driver-oriented controls. The F8 Tributo balances power, design, and usability, making it a popular modern supercar.

Ferrari 488 Pista

The Ferrari 488 Pista is a track-focused evolution of the 488, designed to deliver maximum performance with reduced weight and enhanced aerodynamics. Its 3.9-liter twin-turbo V8 produces around 710 horsepower, making it one of Ferrari’s most powerful V8 cars. The Pista includes racing-derived technologies, such as improved airflow systems, lightweight materials, and precise suspension tuning. It accelerates from 0–100 km/h in about 2.8 seconds and delivers exceptional cornering ability. The interior features minimalist, performance-oriented elements with carbon fiber throughout. The 488 Pista is considered one of Ferrari’s finest driver’s cars, built to excel on both road and track.
